IDEA 9106 · Semester 1, 2024

Event Planning & Experience Design — Cupventure

Semester 1, 2024 · Completed · Group project (4 members)
Completed Service Design UX Research Sustainability Prototyping

Project overview

The challenge

On the University of Sydney campus, single-use bubble tea cups were generating significant waste. Research revealed that while most students had sustainability awareness, a key psychological barrier — feeling their individual actions were insignificant — prevented them from acting. Cupventure was designed around this insight: a gamified cup-return system combining physical stamp cards and digital points to make every recycling action feel visible, rewarding, and meaningful.

Experience journey

01
Before entering
Browse nearby stores via app
02
Order & choose cup
Single-use (+$1) or reusable (free + rewards)
03
Enjoy drink
Cup QR code linked to account
04
Find return bin
App navigates to nearest bin
05
Scan & return
Scan cup QR, drop in bin
06
Earn & redeem
Stamp card or digital points, 6 = free drink

My contribution

User interviews (3 participants)
Stakeholder mapping
Usability test notetaker (4 sessions)
Research plan writing
Consent forms & observation coding

STAR reflection

Situation

Bubble tea consumption on USYD campus was generating large amounts of single-use plastic waste. Research found that students had environmental awareness but lacked convenient recycling infrastructure and immediate feedback to translate awareness into action.

Task

As one of four team members, I was responsible for conducting 3 user interviews, drawing the stakeholder map, taking notes across all 4 usability test sessions, and writing the individual research plan.

Action

I recruited and interviewed participants from different cultural backgrounds to gain diverse perspectives. During usability testing, I served as the systematic notetaker across all 4 sessions, capturing user confusion points and helping the team translate feedback into clear iteration directions.

Result

Through two rounds of iteration, the prototype evolved from a paper stamp card with QR codes to a complete system concept incorporating AR reward visualisation, an environmental credit score, and smart auto-sensing bins. The project deepened my understanding of behaviour-centred design.

Key findings from user testing

Pain point 1

Users didn't understand the app's purpose — clear onboarding slogans were needed.

Pain point 2

Bin locations were unclear — users assumed return points only existed inside stores.

Pain point 3

Manual scanning felt slow — users wanted automatic sensing bins for faster returns.

Pain point 4

The coexistence of physical stamps and digital points confused users — a clear choice prompt was needed at ordering.

Design iteration

User feedback

"What is this about?" — didn't understand app purpose

Root issue

No clear onboarding or purpose explanation

Iteration

Add clear slogans in-store and on app home screen

User feedback

"It feels slow to scan manually every time"

Root issue

Too much friction in the return process

Iteration

Design smart auto-sensing bins for automatic recognition

User feedback

"Without a penalty system, won't people just keep the cups?"

Root issue

No accountability mechanism

Iteration

Introduce an environmental credit score (starting 100), balancing incentive with accountability
